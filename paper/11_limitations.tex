\section{Limitations}

The method is still a 2.5D compromise. Deep stacks of independently important surfaces may require `K>2` or full volumetric methods. Support logic, bridging heuristics, and other slicer assumptions are inherited from planar slicing and can become inaccurate when the real-space layer geometry curves substantially. Firmware planners also impose practical limits on move density, so postprocessing should avoid unnecessary re-segmentation.
