\section*{Abstract}

Heightfield-based non-planar slicing is attractive because it is practical: it avoids tetrahedralization, large volumetric optimization problems, and fragile solver configuration. Its main structural limitation is that a single heightfield can only target one surface family along each vertical column, which leaves internal ceilings, recessed shelves, and other multi-depth features outside direct control. We introduce FlexSlicer, a rank-2 depth-varying displacement field that preserves the lightweight least-squares character of heightfield methods while enabling explicit control over a second target surface family. The method extracts top-hit and second-hit targets, solves two anchor heightfields, blends them using a height-only schedule with closed-form manufacturability checks, and integrates with mainstream slicers through a warp / slice / unwarp pipeline. We also show that anisotropy-aware objective terms can be added without leaving the least-squares regime. The resulting pipeline bridges the gap between single-heightfield practicality and volumetric multi-depth control.
