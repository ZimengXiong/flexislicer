\section{Comparison}

FlexSlicer occupies a specific operating point. Compared with planar adaptive height, it can curve interfaces within a layer. Compared with QuickCurve, it can explicitly target a second surface family. Compared with CurviSlicer, it avoids tetrahedralization and reuses standard slicer infrastructure.

The claim is not that FlexSlicer dominates volumetric methods in general. The claim is narrower and more useful: for shallow multi-depth features where two surface families matter most, a rank-2 field captures the missing capability at much lower toolchain cost.
